Leaving the aerospaceÂ¶

Aeroplanes can legally leave the aerospace only through gates.

Failing to leave the aerospace from a gate will trigger a penality as described in the scoring section of the manual. Leaving the aerospace from a different gate than the designated, will also trigger a penalty, although not as severe.

Gates are bi-directional which means planes can both enter and exit the aerospace. To avoid collisions, in ATC-NG, eaven fligh levels (like 10, 20, 30) are reserved for leaving the aerospace, while odd ones (like 05, 15, 25) are reserved for entering it. Leaving the aerospace from the correct gate but the wrong fligh level will count as leaving the aerospace at the wrong gate.
